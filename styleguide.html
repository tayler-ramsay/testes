Grounded in data | Elevated by collaboration

After some research, it quickly became apparent that the process of building websites and, well, buildings is pretty similar. Both go through intensive planning to ensure that the structure’s multiple functions seamlessly work together. In the beginning, both are rigorously researched to ensure they’re a sound investment. And, finally, both require a large, collaborative team to make it all happen.

With so many procedural overlaps between our two industries, we took the initiative of interpreting our process into the five stages of architectural design. If you don’t use the five stages, then please view this as a thinly veiled attempt to relate #fail.

01

Schematic design —

discovery

As in architecture, step one is defining the shape and size of the project: the objective, the requirements and any anticipated issues. The result is a project blueprint, outlining the website’s specs, sitemap, content plan, schedule and budget. This will guide both our team and CRA — ensuring clarity and efficiency throughout.

02

Design development —

envision

At this stage, we start gathering materials, refining the plan and landing on a final concept. Instead of interior finishing options and structural engineering, our collected research from the Discover step translates into diagrams and schematics that advise the overall structure of the website.

03

Construction documents —

elaborate

This is the technical part. Similar to architects and engineers blowing out detailed construction documents, our designers and coders begin to create the actual site from the agreed-upon concept. Research and strategy from earlier stages are leveraged to create a high-fidelity view of the final website.

04

Bidding —

billing

This is the stage where our tidy analogy begins to break down, but both bidding and billing deal with financials, so please bear with us. At this point, things are wrapping up — quality testing has been completed, the site is up, payment is made and drinks are in order.

We’ve attached a downloadable PDF at the bottom of this page, where you can find a detailed breakdown of this project’s scope.

05

Construction administration —

evolve

Like an architect periodically checking in on his or her project’s job site, we offer continuing maintenance services post-build. The Evolve phase begins as soon as the website is launched, to ensure that content is always current, experience is always relative and technology is always stable.

Social frameworks

Is a bank a building or is it a specific set of financial interactions? What about a hospital? Is it the brick and mortar or the nurses and physicians that define it? By themselves, buildings are simply hollow structures. But once they’re filled with human activity, they take on a life of their own.

As architects, you have the power to create a physical ecosystem that enhances your project’s intended function. Because with the right floor plan, an airtight data center can help its employees breathe easy. And a nature center’s building materials can practice what its contents preach.

For over 30 years, CRA has been changing the architectural landscape by designing not buildings, but beautifully functional social frameworks.

Harmonious Design

Like a building’s front facade, roof and walls, the art of architecture has many sides. The client’s wants. The designer’s vision. Functionality. Artistic license. Environmental concerns. You get the point.

For a project to be completed — where the process is smooth and the end result is beautiful — everything must work in harmony. What sets CRA apart is its 30-plus-year track record of ensuring that every aspect of architecture is given its due, with the keystone being the client’s voice and needs.

CRA’s current brand position of a client-oriented approach touches on this idea. You already create stunning projects where the client, the design, the environment and your firm all seamlessly work together. “Harmonious Design” simply elevates the look and language of this position.
